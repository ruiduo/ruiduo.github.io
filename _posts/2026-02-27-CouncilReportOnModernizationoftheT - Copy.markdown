Dear Riders of the T,

Due to increasing demand, We have plans in place to expand the number of Green Lines serving the Boston Metropolitan Area. We know the forty frequent riders of the Green Line, or so our ticket sale data tells us, will greatly appreciate this expansion. We will continue assembling the Green Line in the awkward claw-like shape raking across the City. Our Designation Committee has decided they will be named, in accordance with Green Line tradition, with the alphabet, and when We run out of letters, We'll probably just slap some Cyrillic on there. The Council has acknowledged your complaints, and yes, they will all still feed into Park street, no matter how contortionist the detour they need to take to get there. 

The Council has noted that the decoupling of Ashmont-Mattapan cars has been confusing to some commuters. In order to aid the identification of whether your car is heading to Ashmont or Mattapan, the committee has ruled all prime cars will head to Ashmont while all composite cars will head to Mattapan. A local red line riding Harvard student may aid you in your selection of car [they will typically self identify at the beginning of each ride].

Our Designation Committee has been hard at work naming the stops on the newly extended Blue Line. Previously, the Blue Line ended at Wonderland, and with months of deliberation and platters of taxpayer fromage, the new terminus will now be called Mollypalooza.

The Charlie Card system has begun to age, and to properly reflect this, the Committee will begin to phase it out. To replace it, the Committee is launching the new Charles Card. Members of the public may attend the launch party, but you should probably just come punctually with a taxi. 

We have upgraded the Red Line rolling stock to reach peak speeds of 50 mph on certain portions of the rail. Now you can text your dinner date that you'll only be 15 minutes late when you both know it'll be the whole half hour. 

The Committee has found the investment in recording the Braintree train announcements in a new sexy suave Chad voice has increased female ridership by as much as 5%. That alpha was discovered via a 500 million dollar contract with the Boston Consulting Group.

The Council has been searching for a solution to reduce the amount of stopping on the above ground Green Line portions. We recognize this upsets passengers who believe that it is finally their time to get off the godforsaken Green Line Car only to realize the train was only stopping for PED XING. To address this, We've implemented a new procedure for conductors across the board: They can just run them dirty walkers the fuck over.

Fare evasion has been a constant issue on the above ground portions of the Green Line. Now, along with the MBTA officers geared in complete military issue kits, We have begun constructing impenetrable walls and a roof covering the track to funnel riders into dedicated ticket payment gates. The Council believes this is true innovation in the scientific Subway community.

And to the riders, We will do anything but build perpendicular tracks to improve connectivity. Progress is measured in track length growing outward from the city. Have you ever wanted to make it to New York on the T itself? Well in maybe a hundred years you can. You will be able to reach anywhere from Boston on the T except somewhere else in Boston. 

Perfect to a T,
MBTA Oversight Council
