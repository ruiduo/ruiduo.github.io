Dear Riders of the T,

Due to increasing demand, We have plans in place to expand the number of Green Lines serving the Boston Metropolitan Area. We will continue assembling the Green Line in the awkward claw-like shape raking across the City. Our Designation Committee has decided they will be named with the alphabet in accordance with Green Line tradition, and when We run out of letters, We’ll probably just slap some Cyrillic on there. The Council takes pride in the accessibility of every Green Line train at Park Street, and thus We've proposed to continue funneling all new Green Line trains into the Park Street Station, no matter how contortionist the detour they need to take to get there. [15 million]

Fare evasion and the safety of passengers is a top concern for the Council. To improve rider safety, We plan on tripling the number of armed officers present in every station. [244 million]

Upon further investigation of the frequent stopping of the above ground portion of the Green Line at intersections and pedestrian crossings, We've found the tourist portion of ridership to really appreciate these moments to take pictures of historic Boston sights. Thus, We've implemented an across the board policy for Green Line conductors to stop whenever they feel like it. [100 thousand]

The Council has noted that the decoupling of Ashmont-Mattapan cars has been confusing to some commuters. In order to aid the identification of whether your car is heading to Ashmont or Mattapan, the committee has ruled all prime cars will head to Ashmont while all composite cars will head to Mattapan. A local red line riding Harvard student may aid you in your selection of car [they will typically self identify at the beginning of each ride]. The first car, being neither a prime nor composite number, will uphold the Red Line tradition of just going wherever the fuck it wants really. [370 million]

Our Designation Committee has been hard at work naming the stops on the newly extended Blue Line. Previously, the Blue Line ended at Wonderland, and with months of deliberation and platters of taxpayer fromage, the new terminus will now be dubbed Mollypalooza. [8 million]

The Charlie Card system has begun to age, and to properly reflect this, the Committee will begin to phase it out. To replace it, the Committee is moving all of the exact same features to the brand new Charles Card. [1.1 million]

The Committee renegotiated another five year contract at double the price with the Boston Consulting Group to identify inefficiencies in the T. In the past decade, BCG has recommended the removal of garbage receptacles from stations. However upon investigation, the Council found that T stations never had any trash cans to begin with. [435 million]

The general ridership of the above ground Green Line has too long been defrauded of the charming ambience of the dim underground. The Council has secured a contract to begin constructing impenetrable walls and a roof to enclose the entire Green Line. [521 million] 

And to the riders, progress is measured in track length growing outward from the city, and by that metric, the Boston T is miles ahead of any other subway system. Have you ever wanted to make it to New York on the T itself? Well in maybe a hundred years you can. Recently, the Council began breaking ground on a brand new T Line that goes 36 miles straight into the Atlantic. With these changes, you will be able to reach anywhere from Boston on the T except somewhere else in Boston. [hopefully 500 thousand]

Perfect to a T,

MBTA Oversight Council
